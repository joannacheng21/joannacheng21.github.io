Introduction/Background

We all depend on crops as a major part of our global food supply, and as a result, the swift detection of crop diseases is essential to maximizing the output of our fields and ensuring the health of the ingredients used in everyday products. However, manual detection of plant diseases is tedious and slow; during the process of identification, disease continues to propagate rapidly. It also requires a high level of expertise, and even so, can be highly error prone [3]. Many of these reasons have spurred research efforts to use machine learning techniques, like random forest classifiers, to identify specific types of disease in certain crops in order to aid in quick prevention of the spread of harmful blights [2]. Other papers have ventured beyond traditional machine learning methods; one group used CNNs to classify a dataset of diseased and healthy plant leaves to achieve a 99.3% accuracy [1]. We plan to use the PlantVillage dataset, which contains 54,303 images of healthy and diseased plant leaves from 13 species. It can be found here.

Problem Definition

We aim to develop a machine learning model that can differentiate between whether a given leaf image is healthy or unhealthy across a variety of species and diseases.

Preprocessing Methods

Contrast Enhancement We can utilize the scikit-image library (more specifically, the rescale_intensity method) to increase the contrast in the plant images, which would make it easier for our CNN model to identify the plants from the background. Smoothing edges We can utilize the gaussian function within the skimage.filters module to smooth edges and remove noise from the plant leaf images. Using the gaussian function on the plant images would remove any unnecessary features that don't correlate with disease in the plant leaves, helping our ML algorithms perform better. Grayscale We can utilize the rgb2gray method from the skimage.color module to convert the colored plant leaf images to grayscale. Training our machine learning model with both colored as well as grayscale images would help the model identify disease utilizing the color of the leaf as well as other important features (such as lesions/spots).

3+ ML Algorithms/Models Identified

Convolutional Neural Network We can train a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) through TensorFlow by using Conv2D and MaxPooling2D layers. First, the image features will be extracted with convolutional layers. Then, with the FC layers and feature information, the model will conclude whether the leaf is from a healthy or unhealthy plant. Decision Tree Classifier The DecisionTreeClassifier class from Scikit can be used to classify leaf data into classes of being healthy or unhealthy. Scikit used the CART (Classification and Regression Trees) algorithm to back its decision tree classifier, which diverges from the C4.5 algorithm in that it doesn’t compute rule sets. A Bagging Classifier We could use sklearn.ensemble.BaggingClassifier to classify our data. This classifier works by choosing randomized subsets of data to classify. Then, by combining the predictions from the randomized subsets, the classifier forms a final prediction. By randomizing, this classifier is useful since it reduces variance, helping reduce overfitting.

3+ Quantitative Metrics

Accuracy: Measure the proportion of correctly identified diseased plants: metrics.accuracy_score(y_true, y_pred, *[, ...]) Precision: Measure proportion of correctly identified diseased plants out of all plants identified as diseased (true positives/true positives + false positives): metrics.average_precision_score(y_true, ...) Recall: Measure proportion of correctly identified diseased plants out of all actually diseased plants (true positives/true positives + false negatives): metrics.recall_score(y_true, y_pred, *[, ...]) F1 Score: Harmonic mean of precision and recall: metrics.f1_score(y_true, y_pred, *[, ...]) Other options: Confusion Matrix, ROC AUC Score

Project Goals

In this project, our goal is to develop an effective model to identify diseased and healthy plants. We hope our model will have a classification accuracy above 90% detection for healthy vs unhealthy. We aim for precision and recall rates above 0.90 to indicate less false positives and negatives as well as resulting F1 Score around 0.90 to indicate a balance in the precision and recall.
